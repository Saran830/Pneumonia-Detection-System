
# AI for Lung Cancer Diagnosis – Literature Review

This literature review explores the role of **Artificial Intelligence and Deep Learning in lung cancer diagnosis**.

## Overview

Lung cancer remains one of the leading causes of cancer-related deaths worldwide. Recent advancements in **AI and Deep Learning**, particularly Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), have shown strong potential in improving early detection and diagnostic accuracy.

This literature review examines:

* Deep learning models used for lung cancer detection
* Ensemble learning techniques
* Explainable AI (XAI) for clinical interpretability
* Challenges in clinical implementation
* Ethical concerns and limitations of AI in healthcare

## Key Topics Covered

* CNN-based lung cancer detection
* Ensemble deep learning approaches
* Model robustness and adversarial training
* Bias and dataset diversity issues
* Explainable AI for clinical decision support
* Ethical implications of AI in healthcare

## Purpose

The goal of this review is to analyse current research trends, identify key challenges, and highlight future research directions for applying AI in medical imaging and lung cancer diagnosis.
